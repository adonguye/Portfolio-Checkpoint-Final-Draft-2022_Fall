Local Beaches and Animals

St. Croix in of the United States Virgin Islands

~~~~~~~~~~~~

Almost every week, I saw a new beach and got to experience sunsets I'll never forget. Sometimes while lying in the sand, I would have cute beach dogs run up to me. On other days, I would collect sea glass in the shallow waters.

St. Croix has a region of the island with what locals call a rainforest. This was a very remote part of the island, and many people who could live in this area were self-sustaining. This became extremely important as hurricane season came each year. It was interesting coming to St.Croix before hurricane season because I could see the waters get rougher as the summer progressed. This became apparent as I learned how to scuba dive at Cane Bay.

Cane Bay is a beautiful beach that I spent many weekends on because it had so many fishes and was popular among locals. There was a famous dive shop nearby, and I had a lot of fun snorkeling around the water with my friends. I saw a variety of fish and way too many sea urchins under my feet. During one of my dives out in this water, I got scratched up pretty bad, but it didn't hurt because of the salt water.
